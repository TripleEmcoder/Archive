   Charles Joseph Fletcher, of [13]Franklin, New Jersey,^[14][1] is an
   American [15]inventor and businessman, and holder of over seventy
   [16]patents.

[[17]edit] Biography

   He is the president of Technology General Corporation, a small ($2
   million annual revenue) manufacturer of drawn metal products, spray
   coating systems, power mixers, and commercial ice crushing equipment.
   Fletcher penned his autobiography, Quest for Survival, in 2002.
   Fletcher earned a [18]Bachelor of Science ([19]B.S.) degree in
   [20]aeronautical engineering from [21]New York University in 1950. A
   contributor to the [22]X-15 rocket, Fletcher also worked on the test
   version of the [23]Lunar Landing Module, and holds seventeen
   aeronautical patents on vertical lift and rocket
   engines.^[24][2]^[25][3] Fletcher was inducted into the [26]Aviation
   Hall of Fame of New Jersey in 1992 and the [27]New Jersey Inventors
   Hall of Fame in 1993.
