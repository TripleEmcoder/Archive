Albert Macovski

   From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
   Jump to: [5]navigation, [6]search
                         Albert Macovski
      Born    1929
   Occupation Professor of [7]Electrical Engineering (Emeritus),
              [8]Stanford University

   Albert Macovski is an [9]American [10]Professor (Emeritus) at
   [11]Stanford University, know for his many innovations in the area of
   imaging, particularly in the medical field. He has over 150 [12]patents
   and has authored over 200 technical articles. His innovations include
   the single-tube color camera and real-time [13]phased array imaging for
   [14]ultrasound. He has also made significant contributions to
   [15]magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), [16]computerized axial tomography
   (CAT scans), and [17]digital radiography. His honors include Founding
   Fellow of the [18]American Institute of Medical and Biological
   Engineering, the [19]IEEE [20]Zworykin Award, and the gold medal of the
   [21]International Society of Magnetic Resonance. ^[22][1]
