Emily Davenport

   From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
   Jump to: [5]navigation, [6]search
            Emily Goss Davenport
   Citizenship American
    Known for  inventing the electric motor
     Spouse    [7]Thomas Davenport

   Emily Goss Davenport was an inventor from [8]Vermont, [9]United States.
   Emily, with her husband [10]Thomas Davenport, [11]invented an
   [12]electric motor^[13][1] and electric [14]locomotive circa 1834.

   Throughout the process of invention, Emily kept detailed notes and
   actively contributed to the process.^[15][1] Needing to [16]insulate
   the motor's iron core, Davenport cut her [17]wedding dress into strips
   of [18]silk to insulate the wire windings.^[19][2] She is also credited
   with the idea of using [20]mercury as a conductor, enabling the motor
   to function for the first time.^[21][2]
