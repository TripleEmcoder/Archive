Ken Forsse

                      Ken Forsse
     Born    September 25, 1936 (1936-09-25) (age 74)
   Known for Creator of [7]Teddy Ruxpin^[8][1]

   Ken Forsse (born September, 1936) is an [9]American inventor, author,
   and producer who created the hit toy [10]Teddy Ruxpin and the
   subsequent animated series [11]The Adventures of Teddy
   Ruxpin.^[12][2]^[13][3]

   After working for [14]The Walt Disney Company, Sid & Marty Krofft
   Entertainment and other entertainment companies, he founded AlchemyII,
   Inc. One of AlchemyII's first projects was building the animatronic
   characters used on the television shows [15]Welcome to Pooh Corner and
   [16]Dumbo's Circus. Forsse and his team then brought Teddy Ruxpin to
   the marketplace. Teddy Ruxpin became the top selling toy of 1985 and
   1986^[17][4]^[18][5] and was made by [19]Worlds of Wonder.

   In 2005, AlchemyII made an agreement with Toy Manufacturer BackPack
   Toys to have Teddy Ruxpin back on the shelves yet again and the toy
   made a successful return in 2006 before eventually being discontinued
   again in 2010. In addition, the animated TV series was released for the
   first time on DVD in 2008.^[20][6]
