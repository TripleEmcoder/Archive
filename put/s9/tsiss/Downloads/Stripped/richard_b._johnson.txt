   Richard Brian Johnson is the author of the book [13]Abominable Firebug
   ([14]ISBN 0-595-38667-9) which presents a definitive overview of
   inmates' daily life at the [15]Lyman School for Boys. Johnson invented
   the [16]Rubber Ducky antenna^[17][1] while attending this
   [18]Westborough, Massachusetts [19]reform school. Johnson went on to a
   highly successful career as an engineer and inventor.^[20][2] He also
   created the [21]JMODEM file transfer protocol.^[22][3] Johnson has also
   continued to be an activist for [23]Civil Rights issues, and as a
   [24]commercial pilot, has even communicated with the [25]President of
   the United States on issues involving [26]General Aviation.^[27][4]
   Although still employed full time as an Engineer, Johnson has continued
   to give talks to groups interested in learning about the nation's first
   reform school.^[28][5]

[[29]edit] Company founder

   Mr. Johnson founded the Danvers, Massachusetts software company,
   [30]Route 495 Software, LLC in early 2009.^[31][6]^[32][7]
