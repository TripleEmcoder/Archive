John Souther

   From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
   Jump to: [5]navigation, [6]search
              John Souther
   Born March 1, 1816(1816-03-01)
        [7]Boston, Massachusetts
   Died September 12, 1911(1911-09-12)
        [8]Newton, Massachusetts

   John Souther (March 1, 1816 – September 12, 1911) was the founder of
   [9]Globe Locomotive Works, an [10]American [11]steam locomotive
   manufacturing company. In his obituary published in the [12]Newton,
   Massachusetts, Town Crier, he is credited with the invention of the
   [13]steam shovel and [14]steam dredger as well as designing the pattern
   for the fence around [15]Boston Common. On his death in 1911, he was
   survived by a son and a daughter. Souther attended the funeral on May
   4, 1870 of [16]Zerah Colburn, the well-known locomotive engineer and
   journalist, who for a time (circa 1853) was employed by Souther.
