Schuyler Wheeler

   From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
   Jump to: [5]navigation, [6]search

   Schuyler Skaats Wheeler (May 17, 1860 – April 20, 1923) was an
   [7]American [8]engineer who invented the two-blade [9]electric fan in
   1882 at age 22.^[10][1] He was awarded the [11]John Scott Medal of
   [12]The Franklin Institute in 1904.

   Wheeler was born in [13]Massachusetts. His two-bladed electric fan was
   produced by the Crocker and Curtis Electric Motor Company. He married
   Amy Sutton on October 11, 1898^[14][2]. He died of [15]angina pectoris
   at his home in [16]Manhattan. ^[17][3]
