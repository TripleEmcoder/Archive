   Frederick Converse Beach (March 27, 1848 – June 18, 1918), son of
   [11]Alfred Ely Beach, was editor of the [12]magazine [13]Scientific
   American and of the new [14]Encyclopedia Americana in the early 1900s,
   and an inventor of a [15]photolithographic process. ^[16][1]

   Businessman and editor. Born on March, 27 1848, in Brooklyn New York,
   the son of Alfred Ely Beach. Frederick Converse Beach graduated from
   Yale's Sheffield Scientific School in 1868. He entered Munn & Company,
   taking, like his father, a spe- cial interest in inventions and
   patents, and in 1896 became a director of the Scientific American
   Company. He had various other busines inter- ests as well and was
   active in promoting the establishment of the U.S. parcel-post system.
   An early and noted amateur photographer, he founded in 1889 the
   American Amateur Photog- rapher (later American Photography) and served
   as its editor. From 1902 he was also editor in chief of the
   Encyclopedia Americana. Beach died on June 18, 1918, in Stamford,
   Connecticut.
