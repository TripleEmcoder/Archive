   Otis Tufts (February 14, 1804 - November 5, 1869) was a machinist and
   inventor who built the first steam-operated [10]printing press in the
   United States and invented the steam [11]pile driver.^[12][1]

[[13]edit] Biography

   Otis Tufts was born in [14]Cambridge, Massachusetts in 1804 to Stephen
   Tufts and Lucy Frost Tufts. He had a twin brother, Joseph Tufts who
   died on July 24, 1807. On April 1, 1824 he married Sarah Caroline
   Oliver (1803-1868), in [15]Malden, Massachusetts. Sarah was the
   daughter of William Oliver and Sarah Caroline Cheever. Together they
   had four children: Sarah, Eliza, Caroline and Otis.^[16][2]

   In 1837, he built a steam-operated printing press. He built the first
   [17]double-hulled iron [18]steamship and the first vessel constructed
   of all iron in the United States based on the designs of [19]John
   Ericsson. Tufts also invented the steam pile driver. He died in
   Massachusetts in 1869 at the age of 65.^[20][3]
