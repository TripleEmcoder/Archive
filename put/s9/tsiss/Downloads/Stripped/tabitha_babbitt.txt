Tabitha Babbitt

   Tabitha Babbitt (1784–1853) was an early [14]American [15]tool maker
   who is credited with inventing the first [16]circular saw used in a
   [17]saw mill in 1813.^[18][1] She was a member of the [19]Shaker
   community in [20]Harvard, Massachusetts.

   She was watching men use the difficult two-man [21]pit saw when she
   noticed that half of their motion was wasted. The first circular saw
   she made is in [22]Albany, [23]New York. She also shares the invention
   of [24]cut nails with [25]Eli Whitney. As a [26]Shaker, Babbitt never
   [27]patented any of her inventions.
