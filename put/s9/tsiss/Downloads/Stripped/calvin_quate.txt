   Calvin F. Quate was born on 7 December 1923 in [14]Baker, Nevada. He is
   one of the inventors of the [15]atomic force microscope. He is a
   professor of Applied Physics and [16]Electrical Engineering at
   [17]Stanford University.

   He earned his [18]bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from the
   [19]University of Utah [20]College of Engineering in 1944, and his
   [21]Ph.D. from Stanford University in 1950.

   Quate was awarded the 1980 [22]IEEE Morris N. Liebmann Memorial Award
   and the [23]IEEE Medal of Honor in 1988 for "the invention and
   development of the scanning acoustic microscope."^[24][1]

   Quate became a Senior Research Fellow at the [25]Palo Alto Research
   Center (PARC) in 1984.^[26][1]
