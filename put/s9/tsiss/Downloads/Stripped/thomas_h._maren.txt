   Thomas H. Maren (1918 – August 15, 1999) was a professor of medicine at
   the [18]University of Florida. He was the founding father for the
   [19]University of Florida College of Medicine, and he invented
   [20]Trusopt to help people with [21]glaucoma.^[22][1]

[[28]edit] Life and death

   Maren was born in [29]New York City, and he served the war effort in
   [30]World War II. He was educated at [31]Princeton University, and
   received his medical degree from [32]Johns Hopkins University.^[33][2]

   Maren died at the age of 81 of heart failure.

[[34]edit] Education

     * Bachelors degree in chemistry from [35]Princeton University in
       1938.
     * Medical degree from [36]Johns Hopkins University in 1951.

[[37]edit] Awards

     * Hopkins Distinguished Medical Alumnus Award.
     * Honorary Doctorate from [38]Uppsala University.
     * Inducted into the Hopkins Society of Scholars.
