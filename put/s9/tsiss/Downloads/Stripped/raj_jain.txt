   Raj Jain (born 17 August 1951) is a professor of [19]Computer Science
   and [20]Engineering in the [21]Washington University School of
   Engineering and Applied Science at [22]Washington University in St.
   Louis, [23]Missouri.

[[29]edit] Affiliations

   Until 2005 he was the Chief Technology Officer and Co-Founder of Nayna
   Networks, Inc. – a next generation telecommunications systems company
   in San Jose, CA. Prior to that he was a professor of Computer and
   Information Sciences at the [30]Ohio State University in [31]Columbus,
   Ohio and a Senior Consulting Engineer at [32]Digital Equipment
   Corporation in Littleton, Massachusetts. He was also a visiting
   scientist at [33]Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1983, 1985,
   and 1987.

[[34]edit] Research Contributions

   Dr. Jain is the co-inventor of the DEC-bit scheme for [35]congestion
   avoidance in computer networks^[36][1] which has been adapted for
   implementation in Frame Relay networks as forward explicit congestion
   notification (FECN), [37]ATM Networks as Explicit Forward Congestion
   Indication ([38]EFCI), and [39]TCP/IP networks as [40]Explicit
   Congestion Notification(ECN).

   He is also the co-inventor of the Additive Increase Multiplicative
   Decrease ([41]AIMD) principle used for traffic management in computer
   networks and Jain’s fairness Index.^[42][2]^[43][3]

   His work on timeout based [44]congestion control influenced the design
   of the [45]slow start algorithm in [46]TCP/IP networks.^[47][4]
   ^[48][5]

[[49]edit] Publications

   He is author of four books. His second book The Art of Computer Systems
   Performance Analysis published by [50]Wiley Interscience won the 1991
   Best Advanced How-to Book, Systems award from [51]Computer Press
   Association.^[52][6]

[[53]edit] Education

   Dr. Jain obtained a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics ([54]Computer Science)
   from [55]Harvard University, Cambridge, Massachusetts in 1978, an M.E.
   in Automation from [56]Indian Institute of Science Bangalore, India in
   1974, and a B.E. in Electrical Engineering from [57]Awdhesh Pratap
   Singh University, Rewa, Madhya Pradesh, India in 1972.
