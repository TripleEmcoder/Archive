   John Lyon Burnside III (November 2, 1916 – September 14, 2008) was the
   inventor of the [14]teleidoscope, the darkfield kaleidoscope and the
   [15]Symmetricon,^[16][1] and, because he rediscovered the math behind
   kaleidoscope optics, for decades, every maker of optically correct
   kaleidoscopes sold in the US paid him royalties. He was the partner of
   [17]Harry Hay for 40 years, from 1962 until Hay's death in 2002. He
   lived in [18]San Francisco, California,^[19][2] until his death from
   complications of brain cancer on September 14, 2008.^[20][3]

[[27]edit] Early life

   An only child born in [28]Seattle, he was raised by his mother after
   his father left the family; being poor, she periodically placed her son
   in the care of orphanages.^[[29]citation needed]

   He served briefly in the [30]Navy, and settled in [31]Los Angeles in
   the 1940s.

[[32]edit] Gay rights activism

   Burnside and his partner Hay formed a group in the early 1960s called
   the [33]Circle of Loving Companions that promoted gay rights and gay
   love. In 1966 they were major planners of one of the first [34]gay
   parades, a protest against [35]exclusion of homosexuals from the
   military, held in Los Angeles. In 1967, they appeared as a gay couple
   on the [36]Joe Pyne television show.

   In the late 1970s, they founded, along with [37]Don Kilhefner and
   [38]Mitch Walker, the [39]Radical Faeries.^[40][4]

[[41]edit] Personal life

   Burnside married Edith Sinclair in Los Angeles, the pair had no
   children. Burnside later met [42]Harry Hay in 1962 at [43]ONE
   Incorporated; the two fell in love and became life partners. Burnside
   died Sunday, September 14, 2008 at the age of 91. [44][1]
