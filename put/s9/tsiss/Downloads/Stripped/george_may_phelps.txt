   Phelps' Electro-motor Printing Telegraph from circa 1880, the last and
   most advanced telegraphy mechanism designed by George May Phelps
   Born March 19, 1820(1820-03-19)
   [7]Watervliet, [8]New York, [9]U.S.
   Died May 18, 1888(1888-05-18) (aged 68)
   [10]Brooklyn, [11]New York, U.S.
   Nationality [12]American
   Occupation [13]Inventor

   George May Phelps (March 19, 1820 – May 18, 1888) was a 19th century
   American inventor of automated [14]telegraphy equipment. He is credited
   with synthesizing the designs of several existing printers into his
   line of devices^[15][1] which became the dominant apparatus for
   automated reception and transmission of telegraph messages.
