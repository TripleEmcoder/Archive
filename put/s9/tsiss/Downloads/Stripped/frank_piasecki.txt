   Frank Piasecki (pronounced [12]/piːəˈsɛki/, [13]PEE-ə-SEK-ee; October
   24, 1919 – February 11, 2008) was an American engineer and
   [14]helicopter aviation pioneer. Piasecki pioneered [15]tandem rotor
   helicopter designs and created the compound helicopter concept of
   vectored thrust using a ducted propeller.

[[23]edit] Biography

   [24]Wiki letter w cropped.svg This section requires [25]expansion with:
                                 Fill in post-Vertol time.
   A [26]Boeing 234 Chinook and [27]Boeing Vertol 107-II showing the
   tandem rotor design

   Born in [28]Philadelphia, [29]Pennsylvania to an immigrant [30]Polish
   tailor, Piasecki worked for [31]autogyro manufacturers while still in
   high school and studied mechanical engineering at the [32]University of
   Pennsylvania before graduating with a bachelor's degree from [33]New
   York University. In 1940, he formed PV Engineering Forum with former
   Pennsylvania classmate Howard Venzie. He built a single-person,
   single-rotor helicopter designated the [34]PV-2 and flew it on April
   11, 1943. This helicopter impressed the [35]United States Navy
   sufficiently to win Piasecki a development contract.^[36][2]

   The name PV Engineering was changed to Piasecki Helicopter Corporation
   in 1946. After a boardroom dispute, Piasecki left Piasecki Helicopter
   in 1955 and formed the Piasecki Aircraft Company.

[[37]edit] Marriage and children

   Piasecki was married to Vivian O'Gara Weyerhaeuser on December 20,
   1958.^[38][3] They had seven children, Nicole, Frederick, John, Lynn,
   Frank, Michael, and Gregory.

[[39]edit] Death

   Piasecki died at his home on February 11, 2008 after a series of
   [40]strokes. He was 88.

[[41]edit] Awards

     * [42]Philip H. Ward, Jr. Medal from [43]The Franklin Institute in

     * [44]National Medal of Technology - [45]President Ronald Reagan
       presented Piasecki with the country's highest technical honor in
